Coconut Curry Fish

Description:

Coconut curry fish is a flavorful dish that combines the sweetness of coconut milk with the savory taste of curry spices. Fresh fish is typically used for this dish, which is cooked in a creamy coconut sauce along with a variety of vegetables such as onions, peppers, and carrots. The curry flavor comes from a blend of spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, and ginger, which are simmered together with the coconut milk to create a rich and aromatic sauce. The dish can be served over a bed of rice, with naan bread, or alongside a variety of other side dishes such as steamed vegetables or lentils. Coconut curry fish is a popular dish in many cuisines, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Caribbean, and is loved for its bold and exotic flavors.

Ingridents:

  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 pound cod fillets, cut into chunks
  • 1 Â½ tablespoons curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (14 ounce) can coconut milk
  • 1 (16 ounce) package frozen stir-fry vegetables
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

Steps:

  1. Bring the water and rice to a boil in a pot. Cover, reduce heat to low, and simmer 45 minutes.
  2. Heat the oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Stir in the onion and garlic, and cook 1 minute. Mix in cod, and season with curry powder, chili powder, turmeric, cumin, salt, and pepper. Pour in the coconut milk, and mix in frozen vegetables. Cover, and cook 10 minutes, or until vegetables are tender and cod is easily flaked with a fork.
  3. Drain 1/2 cup liquid from the skillet into a bowl, and mix with cornstarch. Stir back into skillet to thicken sauce. Serve over the cooked rice.
